# Ethan Kunz, MBA, B.S. EE
**Senior Electrical Engineer | Technical Consultant | Expert Witness**

## Executive Summary
Senior Electrical and Embedded Systems Engineer with over a decade of combined industrial, defense, and national-security engineering experience. Mr. Kunz provides expert analysis and testimony in matters involving electronic product failures, embedded systems defects, PCB design and manufacturing disputes, industrial electrical accidents, high-voltage system incidents, biomedical equipment malfunctions, and patent infringement claims in the electrical engineering domain. His unique combination of a B.S. in Electrical Engineering (Cum Laude) and an MBA equips him with both the technical depth to perform forensic-level hardware analysis and the business acumen to quantify economic damages and assess standard-of-care violations. As a decorated U.S. Army Veteran who served as a Biomedical Equipment Technician performing failure analysis on life-critical medical hardware, Mr. Kunz brings a disciplined, methodical approach to every engagement.

## Basis for Expert Opinions
Mr. Kunz's opinions are grounded in over ten years of direct, hands-on engineering across multiple high-consequence industries: national security systems at Honeywell FM&T / Kansas City National Security Campus, heavy industrial electrical systems at Nucor Corporation, and life-saving device design at Global Rescue Systems. He holds four patent-level innovations — three disclosures accepted by Honeywell in Li-Fi optical communications and software firesets, and one personally-owned patent in millimeter-wave radar technology. His military service as a Biomedical Equipment Technician included forensic-level maintenance, calibration, and failure analysis of FDA-regulated medical devices per 21 CFR Part 820 and MIL-STD requirements. He maintains a professional forensic analysis laboratory equipped with multi-channel logic analyzers, ultrasonic soldering, hot-flow rework stations, and 4K digital microscopy for physical evidence examination of electronic assemblies.
